Fun Facts!

Click on the pictures to see the fun facts and hear the different elephant noises.

African Elephants live in tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests, flooded grasslands, Miombo woodlands, and Acacia savannahs.

African elephants are known to have very short necks. This is often seen as a disadvantage, but their shorter neck is great for supporting their heavy heads. Their heads could be a lot heavier, but luckily an elephant's skull isn't solid. It's filled with air bubbles that add size but not weight.

Because of an African elephant's size, it cannot jump. It must have at least one foot on the ground at all times. They also can't trot or gallop. However, these creates can charge at speeds up to 25 mph.

African elephants have dorsal and ventral 'fingers' at the end of their trunks. They are great for grabbing things and food-gethering.

An African elephants teeth come in sequentially instead of vertically. Because of this, the teeth move forward along the jaw as worn out front teeth are spit out or swallowed. The last tooth to break through will last at least 30 years.
